**Shipped 2026-04-27 · Permissions**

Bower used to be all-or-nothing: anyone in your workspace could see anything
in your workspace. That worked for tight teams. It didn't work when you needed
to share one specific protocol with a collaborator at another lab, or hide a
draft of a manuscript from junior team members.

Every artifact now has its own share dialog.

## Per-artifact access

Open any note, attachment, collection, or project, click **Share**, and pick:

| Role | Can read | Can edit | Can re-share |
|---|---|---|---|
| **View** | yes | — | — |
| **Edit** | yes | yes | — |
| **Manage** | yes | yes | yes |

Roles cascade: granting **Edit** on a project also grants **Edit** on every
note inside it. Override at any level by setting an explicit role on a child.

## What's new in the dialog

- **Search by name or email** to add workspace members or external
  collaborators (external collaborators receive an invite email).
- **Inherited access** is shown next to each member ("via project Foo") so
  you understand *why* someone has access, not just *that* they do.
- **Owner row** is hidden on private artifacts to reduce dialog noise — you
  already know it's yours.

## Why this matters

Real labs don't share evenly. A PI might want full edit access to every
protocol but only view access on individual experiment notes. A collaborator
across institutions might need access to one project and nothing else.
The previous workspace-wide model couldn't express that; this one does.
