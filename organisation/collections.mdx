Collections are how you group related notes in Bower. Use them to organise by experiment, project, protocol run, date range, or whatever structure matches how you work.

## What a collection is

A collection is a named group of notes. Notes can belong to more than one collection — adding a note to a collection doesn't move it, it just associates it.

Collections sit in your page tree on the left-hand side of the desktop app. You can nest them, reorder them, and share them with workspace members.

Or: right-click any existing note and select **Add to collection > New collection**.

## Adding notes to a collection

- **Drag and drop** — drag a note from the main view into a collection in the page tree.
- **Right-click** — right-click any note and select **Add to collection**.
- **Bulk add** — select multiple notes with Cmd+click (Mac) or Ctrl+click (Windows), then right-click and choose **Add to collection**.

## Organising collections

Collections can be nested inside other collections. Drag a collection over another to nest it — useful for organising by project at the top level and experiment within.

To reorder collections, drag them up or down in the page tree.

## Sharing a collection

Collections are visible to all members of your workspace by default. To restrict access to specific members, open the collection and click **Share settings** in the top right.

## Searching within a collection

To search within a specific collection rather than the whole workspace, open the collection first, then use **Cmd+K** or **Ctrl+K**. Search results will be scoped to that collection.
