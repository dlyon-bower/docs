---
title: "Sharing artifacts"
description: "Generate a public link to share an artifact with anyone, no Bower account required."
---

You can share any artifact with collaborators, reviewers, or anyone else who doesn't have a Bower account. Sharing generates a unique link that gives read-only access to that artifact's content. The viewer does not need to sign in.

## Create a share link

1. Open the artifact you want to share.
2. Open the artifact's action menu (the three-dot menu in the toolbar).
3. Select **Share**.
4. In the **Share** modal, click **Create link**.
5. Optionally, set an expiry date. The link will stop working automatically after that date.
6. Copy the link and send it to whoever you want to share with.

Anyone with the link can view the artifact's title and content. They cannot edit the artifact, access your workspace, or see any other artifacts.

## What viewers can see

- The artifact's title
- The full note content, including text, headings, lists, and tables
- Images attached to or embedded in the note

Viewers cannot see your workspace name, your identity as the creator, or any other artifacts in your workspace.

## Manage existing links

To see all active share links for an artifact:

1. Open the artifact's action menu.
2. Select **Share**.
3. The **Share** modal lists all active links, including when each was created and how many times it has been viewed.

## Revoke a link

1. Open the artifact's action menu.
2. Select **Share**.
3. Find the link you want to deactivate.
4. Click **Revoke**.

The link stops working immediately. Anyone who tries to use it will see a "link not found" message. Revocation is permanent — you cannot re-enable a revoked link, but you can create a new one.

## Expiry

When you create a link you can set an optional expiry date. After that date, the link stops working automatically, the same way a revoked link does. If you don't set an expiry, the link remains active until you revoke it manually.

## What is not shared

The following are never visible to someone accessing a shared link:

- Your workspace name or any workspace details
- Your name or any identifying information about who created the link
- Other artifacts in your workspace
- Collections, tags, or any organisational structure
- Internal Bower IDs or system metadata

Share links give access to one artifact only. They are not a way to share your entire workspace or notebook.
