---
title: "Roles and permissions"
description: "Understand what owners, admins, members, and viewers can do in a workspace."
---

Every person in a workspace has a role that determines what they can do. Roles are assigned per workspace — you can be an admin in one workspace and a member in another.

## Role hierarchy

| Role | What they can do |
| --- | --- |
| **Owner** | Everything. Full control over the workspace, including billing, deletion, and transferring ownership. |
| **Admin** | Everything a member can do, plus: invite and remove members, change member roles, update workspace settings. |
| **Member** | Capture, create, edit, and delete notes. Search, use Bird, manage collections and tags. Full day-to-day research access. |
| **Viewer** | Read-only access. Can view notes, collections, and search results, but cannot create, edit, or delete anything. |

## Assigning roles

When you [invite someone](/workspaces-and-account/invite-team-members) to a workspace, you choose their role. Only owners and admins can invite members or change roles.

To change someone's role after they've joined:

1. Go to **Settings > Members**.
2. Find the member.
3. Select their new role from the dropdown.

## Workspace ownership

Every workspace has exactly one owner — the person who created it. Ownership can be transferred to another admin from **Settings > Members**.

If you need to [delete your account](/workspaces-and-account/manage-account#delete-your-account) and you're the owner of a shared workspace, you'll need to transfer ownership first.

## What viewers can see

Viewers have full read access to the workspace. They can:

- Browse notes, collections, and protocols
- Use search and Bird to query the workspace
- View audit logs

They cannot create, edit, delete, import, or capture anything.
