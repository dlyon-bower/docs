Your research data is sensitive. Here's how Bower protects it.

## Workspace isolation

Workspaces are fully isolated from each other. This is enforced at every level:

- **Every API request** is scoped to a single workspace. The server verifies your membership before returning any data.
- **Search results** only include notes from your current workspace.
- **Bird** only accesses notes in the workspace you're working in.
- **Members** of one workspace cannot see data in another workspace unless explicitly invited.

This isolation is not a UI convention — it's enforced by the backend on every request.

## Data storage

- Notes, collections, and metadata are stored in a managed PostgreSQL database.
- Files (images, audio, PDFs, documents) are stored in Google Cloud Storage.
- Authentication is handled by Firebase Authentication.
- All data is hosted on Google Cloud infrastructure.

## What happens when you delete data

- **Deleted notes** are permanently removed along with their associated files. Deletion is not reversible — there is no trash or recovery period.
- **Deleted attachments** are removed from storage alongside their parent note.
- **Removing a member** revokes their access immediately. Notes they created remain in the workspace.
- **Deleting your account** permanently removes your profile and any workspaces you own. This cannot be undone.

<Warning>
Deletion is permanent. If you need to preserve data for compliance or audit purposes, export it first.
</Warning>

## AI processing

When Bower processes your data (voice transcription, photo text extraction, summarisation via Bird), the content is sent to AI model providers for processing. Bower uses:

- Google Cloud services for transcription and text extraction
- AI models for Bird's conversational features

Your data is processed to provide the service and is not used to train AI models.

## Your responsibilities

- Do not store credentials, passwords, or access tokens in notes.
- Use [roles and permissions](/security/roles-and-permissions) to control who can access your workspace.
- Review the [audit logs](/security/audit-logs) periodically if your workspace handles sensitive research data.
